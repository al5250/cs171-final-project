  • The U.S. has more than 393 million guns in circulation — approximately 88.9 guns for every 100 people.
  • The U.S. comprises 5% of the world's population, yet we own 35-50% of every type of gun worldwide.
  • The U.S. has many more guns per capita than any other country.
  • How has this prevalance of guns affected the lives of Americans?

Data from Children's Hospital of Philadelphia "Gun Violence: Facts and Statistics" & Vox: "Gun crime is more prevalent in the US than in other rich countries"

  • Between 1982 and April 2018, 99 mass shootings have occurred in the U.S.
    • In 2017 alone, 11 mass shootings in the U.S. caused 117 fatalities and 587 injuries occurring in concert, religious, workplace, airport, and shopping venues and in the community.
